Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson

The Rock, whose real name is Dwayne Douglas Johnson, was born in Hayward, California, on May 2, 1972. He is the son of professional wrestler Rocky Johnson (born Wayde Douglas Bowles) and Ata Johnson (born Feagaimaleata Fitisemanu). His mother is Samoan (her own father was Peter Fanene Maivia, also a professional wrestler), and his father is black (of Black Nova Scotian origin) from Amherst, Nova Scotia, Canada. Growing up, Dwayne spent a lot of time traveling with his parents and seeing his father's ring shows. Dwayne started playing football in high school, and he quickly won a full scholarship to the University of Miami, where he excelled as a football player. Dwayne's back injury in 1995 prevented him from playing in the NFL. The Canadian League offered him a three-year contract, but he quit after one year to pursue a career in professional wrestling.In the USWA, he made his professional wrestling debut as Flex Kavanah, and he and Brett Sawyer won the tag team championship there. After Dwayne became Rocky Maivia in the WWE in 1996, he joined "The Nation of Domination" and changed his character to a heel. Rocky soon assumed control of the "Nation" and started adopting The Rock's persona. Following the breakup of the "Nation," The Rock became a member of the "Corporation," an even more elite group of wrestlers, and he started an unforgettable fight with Steve Austin. The Rock was soon expelled from the "Corporation". "The Peoples Champion" was his moniker when he changed his visage. The Rock left WWE in 2000 to rehearse for his role in The Mummy Returns (2001). During the WCW/ECW invasion in 2001, he made a comeback and joined a group of WWE wrestlers for The Scorpion King (2002), which was a prequel to The Mummy Returns (2001).Dwayne's ex-wife Dany Garcia gave birth to a daughter named Simone Alexandra Johnson in 2001. He also has two children from his marriage to singer and composer Lauren Hashian: Jasmine, born in 2015, and Tiana Gia, born in 2018.
